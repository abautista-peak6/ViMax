SCENE 1 - INT. SMALL APARTMENT BATHROOM - DAY

Shot 0:
A tired tenant stands in a cramped bathroom beside a tub with a leaking faucet. Water taps steadily into the tub with an annoying rhythm. The tenant sends a quick message to the landlord, trying to stay calm while staring at the drip.
Tenant: "Hey, the tub faucet is leaking pretty badly. Can someone come take a look?"
Audio: steady drip, quiet apartment hum, phone notification sound.

Shot 1:
A rough, unprepared plumber arrives at the apartment door carrying only a small, disorganized tool bag. He looks around the bathroom, kneels by the tub, pokes at the faucet, and quickly realizes he has no parts with him.
Plumber: "Yeah, I gotta run to the hardware store. Fifteen minutes, tops."
Tenant: "Great. Fifteen minutes sounds perfect."
Audio: awkward footsteps, faucet dripping, the plumber's tool bag clanking loosely.

Shot 2:
Time passes in the apartment. The tenant sits on the couch, checks the clock, checks the phone, looks toward the bathroom, then sighs as the drip continues in the distance. The promised fifteen minutes stretches into a full hour.
Tenant: "Fifteen minutes in plumber time is apparently a different measurement."
Audio: ticking clock, distant faucet drip, low comedic music cue.

SCENE 2 - INT. SMALL APARTMENT BATHROOM - LATER

Shot 0:
The plumber finally returns and starts replacing the tub faucet, but the repair immediately goes sideways. He freezes, peers into the wall, and admits the problem is deeper: the pipes behind the tub need work. He cuts a large rough hole in the wall behind the tub, leaving drywall dust everywhere.
Plumber: "So... the faucet is not really the problem. The pipes behind it are the problem."
Tenant: "That sentence feels expensive."
Audio: saw buzzing, drywall cracking, dusty debris falling, the faucet still dripping.

Shot 1:
The plumber makes another trip to the hardware store, returns an hour later with pipe parts, fixes the leak, then realizes the tub faucet handle also needs replacing. The tenant watches, exhausted, as the plumber casually announces one more trip.
Plumber: "Good news, the leak is fixed. Bad news, I need a new handle."
Tenant: "Let me guess. Hardware store?"
Plumber: "Hardware store."
Audio: wrench turning, water shutoff squeak, brief triumphant silence, then a deflating comedic sting.

Shot 2:
Another hour later, the plumber returns, attaches the new handle, tests the faucet, packs up, and leaves quickly. The tenant enters the bathroom to inspect the work. The faucet is finally quiet, but a huge uncovered hole remains in the wall, surrounded by drywall dust and debris. The tenant stares at the mess in silent disbelief.
Tenant: "Technically fixed is doing a lot of work here."
Audio: door closing, sudden quiet, one tiny piece of drywall crumbling to the floor, deadpan comedic end beat.
